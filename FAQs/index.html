About Mira

Questions about the planet and its regions.

What's so special about Mira?

Well, there's just something about this planet.

Mira is 155 square miles of exhilaration! You get to explore not one, but SIX different regions. From Oblivia's dry climate, to Cauldros' volcanic terrain, there's something for everyone.

What does Mira look like?

This is a very broad question. It would be better to ask what each region looks like instead, since they are all so different. Feel free to learn more about the different regions by visiting their dedicated pages.

What regions does Mira have?

Primordia, Noctilum, Oblivia, Sylvalum, and Cauldros!

Are there any maps available for navigation?

We can provide you with this map of the planet.

You can easily access it via your comm device.

Travel Information

Questions about travel to and from Mira.

How do you get there?

You'll be traveling through space, along with the other lucky passengers. However, no one actually knows where Mira is. Trust us when we say that you'll get here. Eventually.

What should I pack?

Bring anything from Earth that you think you'll miss while you're on Mira. The more personal (photos of loved ones, journals, mementos, etc.), the better.

How long does the trip take?

Approximately two years?

Can you return to Earth once you're on Mira?

This is a one-way trip. You can't return to Earth. We're stuck on Mira forever.

Transportation

Questions about transportation on Mira.

How can I get around while on Mira?

Either by walking/running everywhere or by Skell. Cars are not recommended outside of NLA.

What is a Skell? How can I get one?

A Skell is a giant mech. In its bipedal form, you can walk around or fly in it. It can also transform into a vehicle! All of these forms make it easier for you to travel around Mira.

However, you can only get a Skell if you join BLADE and pass the Skell License Certification Test.

What's the best way to navigate Mira's terrain without a Skell?

Walking, jumping, or running around. Just make sure you don't get noticed by high-level indigens!
